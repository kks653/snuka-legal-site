Snuka Studio

Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 19, 2026

Overview

Snuka Studio creates vintage photo booth portrait packs from photos you choose. This policy explains what data we collect, why we use it, how long we keep it, and how you can request deletion.

Data We Collect

  • Portrait photos you select: source photos uploaded for face-visibility validation and the requested server-side vintage rendering.
  • Generated portraits: preview and full-resolution outputs created from your request.
  • User identifiers: anonymous Supabase user IDs and RevenueCat app user IDs used to own sessions and purchases.
  • Purchase and entitlement data: purchase state needed to unlock full-resolution packs.
  • Safety reports: when you report a generated print, we collect your anonymous Supabase user ID, the generated asset and generation job references, a required reason (sexual content; hateful or harassing; violence or self-harm; privacy or identity; misleading or spam; or other unsafe content), optional details of up to 1,000 characters, report status, and submission time.
  • Crash and performance diagnostics: Sentry diagnostics used to find startup, purchase, export, and generation failures.

How We Use Data

We use your data to provide app functionality: validate portrait quality, create requested photo packs, show previews, unlock paid assets, process deletion requests, prevent abuse, and troubleshoot reliability issues. Safety report data routes a private request to Snuka operations for review and helps limit duplicate or abusive submissions.

AI Processing

Source portraits may be sent to OpenAI through our server-side worker for face-visibility and portrait validation. Production image rendering then uses Snuka's server-side vintage compositor. We do not use your portraits for advertising or cross-app tracking. We do not sell your portraits or generated images.

Generated Content Reports

The in-app Report action lets you select one safety reason and optionally provide up to 1,000 characters of context. The report is a private request for operational review. Submitting it does not automatically classify, remove, block, or regenerate the reported print, and it does not promise a particular review outcome. Do not include unnecessary sensitive information in the optional details.

Processors

We use Supabase for authentication, database, Edge Functions, and private storage; OpenAI for source portrait face validation; RevenueCat for purchase and entitlement state; and Sentry for crash and performance diagnostics.

Retention

Source portrait files are eligible for cleanup 24 hours after a terminal generation or deletion state. Generated asset files and rows are eligible for cleanup after 90 days. A safety report is linked to its generated asset row and is removed when that row is removed, including through session deletion, account deletion, or scheduled generated-asset cleanup.

Session deletion removes the session's known source and generated files from private storage and deletes generated asset rows and their related safety reports. The session, source-photo, and generation-job database rows remain as deleted-state operational records until account deletion or another database cleanup. Account deletion is a separate action: it removes the anonymous Supabase Auth user, user-facing app rows, private storage objects, and Snuka's stored purchase-event rows. A minimal non-image security tombstone containing raw identifiers and the signed-upload storage path remains only to remove an object recreated by a previously issued two-hour upload token; it is purged after token expiry and the next successful storage cleanup. App stores and processors may retain purchase, transaction, security, or diagnostic records under their own retention rules or when required for accounting, disputes, security, or law.

Security

Source portraits and generated assets are stored in private buckets. The app uses signed upload and download URLs. Full-resolution asset URLs are issued only after server-side entitlement checks.

Your Choices

You can remove only the current photo session with the pack deletion control, or delete the anonymous account and its user-facing app data with the separate account deletion control, subject to the temporary upload security tombstone described above. You can also request deletion at Delete Data or by emailing privacy@snukastudio.com.

Children

Snuka Studio is not directed to children under 13. Do not upload photos of another person unless you have the right and consent to do so.

Contact

For privacy questions, contact privacy@snukastudio.com.
